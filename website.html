1. Introduction

The ICT Joint Group Assignment (JGA) explores sustainable garden projects to promote food security, community involvement, and digital literacy. This project aligns with the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDG 1 & 2).

2. Goals

  • Investigate food-security promoting community garden initiatives.
  • Understand obstacles and success factors of community gardening.
  • Create a clean, interactive website to communicate findings.
  • Promote ICT-based problem solving and digital literacy.

3. Approach

Research used government publications, NGO reports, academic papers, and case studies from community projects across South Africa.

4. Research Results

  • Soweto School Garden: Saves the school R2,500/month by providing daily meals.
  • Durban Community Cooperative: Supports 35 households through vegetable sales.
  • Pretoria University Garden: Supplies campus cafeteria and shelters.
  • Limpopo Household Gardens: 120+ households growing beans and maize.
  • Cape Town NGO Project: Donates produce to shelters.

5. Visuals & Data Analysis

  • Soweto School Garden: 950 kg/year
  • Durban Community Garden: 1,300 kg/year
  • University Garden: 600 kg/year
  • Limpopo household gardens: 5,400 kg/year
  • Cape Town's NGO Garden: 700 kg/year

Home gardens contribute the largest share of local food supply in the studied communities.

6. Website Overview

This website uses modern HTML, CSS and JavaScript to present results in an interactive layout. It is designed to be mobile-friendly, accessible, and easy to host on GitHub Pages.

7. Suggestions

  • Encourage small food gardens at household level.
  • Promote rainwater harvesting to reduce water stress.
  • Run training sessions on eco-friendly gardening techniques.

8. Conclusion

Community gardens show promise in addressing poverty and hunger. Collaboration between individuals and organizations—supported by digital skills—can boost sustainable food security.
